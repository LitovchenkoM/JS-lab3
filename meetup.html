NYC Mobile App Developers (iPhone, Droid, iPad)

Address: 222 Broadway
ARcade 8: VR in Motion and Oculus Touch

Note:  RSVP on Meetup does not count as an actual RSVP.  Please make sure to RSVP on Eventbrite

This is a special night. Besides being the last ARcade event of 2016, we will be introducing some new content that will change the world from 2017 and beyond. While introducing new ideas and concepts in VR not yet introduced in our VR series.

The immersiveness of VR content is dependent on how movement is integrated into the VR experience. The content we will showcase will include how motion is being used with VR being used from motion devices like bicycles, to how the new Oculus Touch, to how VR can be used to feel music.

VR in Motion

We will have a cowboy experience that is meant to give people the experience what it is like to ride a horse with help of a bicycle.

Athena 

Described as the world's most immersive gun controller you will get to demo this product currently on Indiegogo in it's prototype stage

https://www.indiegogo.com/projects/athena-by-ilium-vr/x/1072935#/

Oculus Touch

Less than one week old we will have experiences to showcase the power of the Oculus Touch and how it can be Oculus way to overpower the HTC Vive.

Sub-Pac

SubPac is a wearable device that is used for musicians across the world to help one feel the vibration and sounds of music.

The Martian

Will have the Playstation VR to show an experience based on the movie, The Martian, about what it is like to roam on Mars.

Rogue State

Rogue State is a 360 VR film with a found footage style (Example: Paranormal Acitivity, Blair Witch Project) storytelling format. That can be described as a modern day tribute to suspense and horror films of the 1970s like Deliverance, Last House on the Left, and Texas Chainsaw Massacre. While having a socially concious purpose to provide awareness about the influx of hate crimes perpetrated by alt-right America.

More experiences pending based on presenters

N-Languages

Address: 270 Greenwich Street
Another address: Greenwich & Warren Streets (Lower Manhattan/Tribeca)
Morning Algorithms w N-Lang Gang

Collaborate in the study of m algorithms in n languages!


Taking cues from Coursera's Stanford Algorithms course, we meet to code, chat, study, and learn the basics of algorithm design and analysis. We try to focus on some particular algorithms each week, but invite everyone to work at a pace that works for them.

Learners of all levels are welcome!


Join the N-Lang NYC slack group and say hi in the #algorithms channel :)
